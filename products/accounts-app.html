Core Financials

Chart of Accounts & Voucher Types

A fully customizable Chart of Accounts (COA) forms the backbone of your financial structure. The system supports all standard voucher types to handle every transaction scenario.

  • Chart of Accounts

    Multi‑level COA with parent‑child hierarchies, account codes, and detailed descriptions. Supports all standard account types — Assets, Liabilities, Income, Expenses, and Equity.

  • Journal Voucher

    General entries, adjustments, reversals, and inter‑company transfers with full audit trail and approval workflows.

  • Cash Received / Payment Vouchers

    Instant cash settlement with receipt/ payment recording and real‑time cash account updates.

  • Bank Received / Payment Vouchers

    Online transfers, wire payments, and bank transactions with bank reconciliation support.

Automation

Auto‑Posting & Split Payments

The system automatically posts financial entries in real‑time upon sales and purchases, ensuring your general ledger is always up‑to‑date without manual effort.

  • Real‑Time Auto‑Posting

    Sales invoices, purchase invoices, and payment receipts automatically update the general ledger. No manual journal entries required.

  • Split Payments

    Combine Cash, Bank, and Online payment methods in a single invoice. Supports partial payments across multiple channels simultaneously.

  • Multi‑Mode Settlement

    Flexible settlement options — full or partial — with automatic reconciliation across payment modes.

Access Control

Security & Role‑Based Access

Granular Role‑Based Access Control (RBAC) ensures that every user has precisely the permissions they need — and nothing more. Secure your financial data with confidence.

Permission

Define granular, page‑level permission sets with control over:

  • Read — View data and reports
  • Write — Create and update records
  • Edit — Modify existing entries
  • Delete — Remove records
  • Approve — Authorize transactions

Named permission profiles reusable across roles

Role

Create and manage system roles by bundling permission sets. Examples:

  • Finance Manager — Full access to all financial modules
  • Accountant — Create/Edit vouchers, run reports
  • Auditor — Read‑only access to ledgers and reports
  • Approver — Approval authority for vouchers

Assign one or more roles to each user

User

Register and manage system users with:

  • User registration and profile management
  • Role assignment (single or multiple)
  • Active/Inactive status control
  • Password reset and account management

Link users to employee records (optional)

Every action is audited with user, timestamp, and IP logging for complete compliance and accountability.
